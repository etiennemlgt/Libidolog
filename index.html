Tips

  • Consistent and correct use of barrier methods, like condoms, significantly reduces the risk of STD transmission during sexual activity. Ensure the use of high-quality, FDA-approved protection for optimal effectiveness.
  • Schedule routine STD screenings, especially if sexually active or having multiple partners. Open communication about testing with your partner promotes a healthier sexual environment.
  • Vaccines, such as the HPV and Hepatitis B vaccine, offer preventive measures against certain STDs. Discuss vaccination options with your healthcare provider based on age, gender, and sexual activity.
  • Openly communicate with sexual partners about STDs, testing, and prevention methods. Establishing trust and comfort in discussing sexual health promotes a safer environment for everyone involved.
  • Most sexually transmitted diseases are asymptomatic. When symptoms occur they can be non-specific. Accurate diagnostic tests for STIs enlighten the situation.
